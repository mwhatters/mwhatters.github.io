About me.

I was born in Lakeside, San Diego in 1990, son to Barby and Mark Hattersley, adored parents and talented musicians. In San Diego I pursued music & theater arts. Despite my affinity for warm beaches and sunny weather, I left southern California for Santa Cruz, where I obtained a BA in philosophy, among other things. During my studies I developed a vested interested in logic and religious studies. Despite my proclivity for redwood forests, rock climbing and existentialism, I abandoned my natural landscape in favor of the urban grind that is San Francisco. A resident since 2013, my latent interest in logic has resurfaced, compelling me to study computer logic & programming. My desire for becoming a skilled programmer is amplified by my creative desires -- to make something utilitarian, ethically conscious, and inspiring using the frameworks developed by those much smarter than I. I liken it to using an instrument, taking advantage of the notes and chords, all already discovered, to invent a progression not heard prior. The beauty of programming is that one can find such elegance in the logic of a method, much like the logic of music. I intent to play my programming.
