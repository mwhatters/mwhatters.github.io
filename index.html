Welcome to my blog, friends and enemies.

If you are an enemy, LEAVE! Why must you persist?

My name is Marshall. I hope you enjoy the page that you are now visiting. I made it for a school project, as I am part of the Dev Bootcamp experience. I won't lie in saying that making it was very frustrating at times -- CSS can be a very, very painful experience. But hey, no pain, no gain? I think?

Anywho, here is where I'll be posting all of my reflections, blog posts related to the program, and hopefully, in time, some other things related to my other interests. Though for the next couple months, I don't suspect there being any chance to engage in extracurriculars... such is the life.

For the next few weeks during the offsite program, you should expect to see major improvements to this site. This was thrown together in a few hours on a thursday, I suspect I'll have a bit more time to really get the hang of this whole interwebs things.

Index.

Unit one.

  • Why DBC?
    • A brief reflection the decision i've made to abandon comfort in pursuit of something more. An exploration of the first week of offsite-DBC and what I look forward to gaining from this experience.
  • About Version Control
    • A look into the wonders of git and the countless lives and projects it has saved (mine included). An elucidation on distributed version control and what we can do with it to make our coding lives easy.
  • Playing Twister with CSS: Positioning
    • An in depth exploration of positioning declarations with CSS, how to use them, and when to use them. A reflection on surpassing CSS trachery and reaching a zen-like state of positioning mastery.
  • Me & the Abstract
    • Why I learn like I do, and why I like to be such a loner. A tour of my thinking style, and an exploration of the mindsets that can accompany such approaches to learning.
  • The Power of Hashes and Arrays
    • A slightly humorous tour of the Array and the Hash: how they differ from one another, how they are similar, and the incredibly useful methods and features that come equipped with these tools.

Unit Two.

  • Tech XOR Soul
    • A reflection on the tech industry, how it is percieved by my friends and collegues, and some humble pieces of advice suggesting how we can make our lives, as well as everyone elses, a little bit better.

    • A look into the pros and cons of using the powerful ruby method, group_by, as well a discussion on risks of choosing pretty code over uglier, but more viable, solutions.
  • Feedback Loops
    • I like to be candid in my approach to coding, but recently, I've come to discover my own insecurities, problems, and ego attached to my approach and the work I do. This article contains a candid analysis of my pairing style, and how I can improve my emotional skills as a partner and programmer.
